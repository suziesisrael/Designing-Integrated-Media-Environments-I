working to make a difference

Animation

In 2020, Diabetic Ketoacidosis (or DKA) was 2 1/2 times more likely in African Americans than their white counterparts. DKA can lead to coma or even death. Also, DKA in teens strongly correlates to suicidal thoughts and plans.

Process description:
My main challenge was imagery -- since this is a nuanced topic, the images had to be culturally sensitive. My first attempt utilized emojo-like faces to represent the ratio of black to white incidences of DKA, and it had the effect of depersonalization and insensitivity. So I switched to photographs from Adobe Stock to make it more relatable. At the same time, I didn't want to make suicidal ideation about any specific group, so I used illustration to create an abstraction of someone experiencing it.

On the technical side, I used Adobe After Effects with various motion and opacity effects. The "drawing" was accomplished using a technique called "trim paths."
